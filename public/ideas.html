Our Ideas

Jakob Sanderson | President

Demand Better And Give Students A Vote To Leave CFS
Since joining the Canadian Federation of Students, UMSU members have paid them over $4 million. For that price, students deserve an organization that is effective and accountable. Your UMSU believes you should choose where your money goes and that is why we will give students a vote on continued membership. If UMSU decertifies, we will present students with an opportunity to use that money to join another federal advocacy organization and work collaboratively with all provincial student unions - those in and out of CFS - to form a provincial lobbying organization including all Manitoba students for the first time.

Increased Research Employment Opportunities For Students
Your UMSU ready to take advocacy to the next level by employing our best resource; you! We will create a student research jobs program, you can apply for a student research grant though UMSU, where you will be paid a living wage, gain meaningful on campus employment, and shape UMSU’s advocacy priorities. We will also advocate to the federal government to expand the Undergraduate Student Research Award program to social sciences and health research. Last but not least, we will highlight two student researchers each week on our TVs and social media, including at least one outstanding Indigenous student scholar.

Make The University of Manitoba The Destination For Indigenous Education
Located on Treaty 1 Land and housing the National Centre for Truth and Reconciliation, the University of Manitoba should be the flagship University for Indigenous Scholarship. Your UMSU will create an Indigenous Scholar hiring fund, where UMSU’s endowment fund will match contributions made by the university and the province to provide funding for Indigenous instructors. This will ensure that the UofM will offer an Indigenous Language major with streams in Anishinabe, Cree, Oji-Cree, Dakota, Dene, Inuktitut and Michif. We will also lobby the university to increase Indigenous course content and Indigenous scholars in all programs.

Make The UofM And UMSU Leaders In Environmental Sustainability
It is integral that the University of Manitoba takes a leadership role in promoting environmental sustainability. That is we will work with the university to mplement a campus-wide composting program, and incorporate sustainability focused course options and work/study options in all programs. Looking internally, your UMSU will Implement the recommendations of the UMSU Sustainability Working Group that your UMSU President founded this year, including a green events guide, and more accessible composting in our businesses.

Fight For Affordable Education
It is no secret that University is getting more expensive. To fight back, we need experienced leaders with bold and tangible ideas. In addition to mobilizing students against a second straight year of maximum allowable tuition increases, UMSU will build upon this year’s advancements such as increased Open Educational Resources (OERs) in classes and keeping interest off student loans. This year, we will lobby for increased upfront grants, while broadening the access pool to include part time students. As well, we will continue to bring in more affordable classroom materials for students by starting a university wide OER working group with faculty, administration and students, and lobby government to create grant funding for OER creators.

Create More Choice And Opportunity With Your UPass
This is our final year in our current UPass contract, and while many students have enjoyed this access to affordable transit service, it currently does not include the summer semester. Having led preliminary negotiations with Winnipeg Transit this year, your UMSU President will present students with the option of a summer UPass in our next contract. Additionally, we will ensure all students living outside the transit service area can opt out of the UPass program.

Sarah Bonner-Proulx | Vice-President Advocacy

Expanded Campus Safety Initiatives
Sexual violence is a pressing issue - especially here on campus, and we want to ensure that students feel safe and survivors are put first; this is why my team and I will work with the University to implement a Sexual Violence Support and Resource Centre that will act as central hub for both and education and essential services. As well, we will advocate for our faculty and staff to have increased education and training in areas such as diversity and disclosure training , in order to better support our student body. Specifically, Your UMSU will push to ensure there is an increased focus on Indigenous education and having this embedded in any and all training offered. Moreover, our team will see to that more students, particularly new students, are trained in available supports, sexual violence basics and procedures via new student orientation.

Improved Access To Mental Health Supports & Accommodations
Five in five students have mental health, but with education costs rising, limited Financial Aid options alongside the everyday stress of being a University student, it's not surprising that students with mental health problems and illnesses have some of the highest non-completion rates of all post-secondary students. My team and I will ensure that students are getting timely and proper supports and accommodations by advocating to all levels of government to increase funding for existing and new mental health initiatives, supports and accessibility tools for students. Moreover, Your UMSU will lobby for changes to the Canadian Student Loans Program (CSLP) to ensure that its definition of ‘permanent disabilities’ reflects and responds to the diverse situations of students, including the accommodation needs of students with mental health problems and illnesses.

Increase Safety & Reduce Costs Of On-Campus Socials
Your UMSU is committed to ensuring that students enjoy their university experience and stay safe while doing so - this is why our team will continue to work with the University to offset costs associated with newly implemented Police Presence requirement by introducing incentivization mechanisms wherein the more proactive and preventive measures a student group or association has in place for their social, the less time (and money) spent on and the more time you can focus on having fun in a safe environment.

Establish More Family Friendly Spaces On Bannatyne And Fort Garry Campuses
Students come from all walks of life and all face different challenges. No matter what your needs are, your UMSU should have the proper supports. That’s why we will work with the University to implement a Daycare program on Bannatyne campus, with reserved spots for Indigenous students and accessible to inner city students so all our members can access quality, accessible child care. As well, we will increase the number of breastfeeding friendly spaces on all campuses to ensure that we are a family friendly University.

Womyns Equity & Inclusion
Your UMSU wants to ensure that all students have access to an equitable and inclusive experience. Your UMSU is committed to increasing the number of gender neutral washrooms on all campuses and ensuring that more washrooms have access to free sanitary products. Both on and off campus, women continue to be underrepresented in leadership roles - especially in male-dominated fields such as business, science and engineering. My team and I will prioritize investing in programs that help womyn find their voices, challenge gender stereotypes, and discover and celebrate their strength.

Increased Pathways For International Students To Join the Workforce
International students bring unique perspectives and culture to campus - even while paying 3x the tuition as domestic students. Your UMSU wants to make sure international students have the proper supports needed to thrive. In the past, UMSU has worked with student unions across Canada to bring a petition to the floor of the House of Commons and lobby legislators to fast track international students into the workforce by allowing co-op work hours to count towards permanent residency requirements. This year we will concert our efforts to have this regulation changed. In order to ensure this has the largest impact possible, we will work with government to eliminate red tape by making international study visas transferable to co-op terms.

Your UMSU understands that leadership opportunities are important for your time on campus and beyond. We will implement professional development workshops on event planning, marketing strategy and sponsorship for leaders across campus to ensure all groups are providing the highest quality programming possible to our students at the University of Manitoba.

In our centennial year students deserve a memorable celebration. Your UMSU will move Frosh to a better off-campus venue and bring in big names so that it is an experience for all students. Your UMSU is committed to enriching your student experience which is why welcome week will be filled with giveaways, contests, information sessions and a speed-mentorship event to connect incoming students with leaders across campus. Welcome Week will leave students feeling prepared and excited for what they year has in store.

Advocate For Virtual Student Cards
Your UMSU understands that students have enough to worry about as is. This is why we will work with the University to implement virtual student cards for your mobile devices. These will allow students access to the Active Living Centre, pay for food on campus using foodbucks and provide discounts at UMSU businesses. These cards will provide ease and convenience to students all across campus.

Provide A Prep-book Rental System Through UMSU
Your UMSU wants all students to pursue their academic dreams without worrying about financial barriers. This is why we will provide a professional program prep-book rental system through the UMSU front desk. Students will have access to MCAT, LSAT & GMAT books free of charge. We will also work with faculties to partner on information sessions for professional programs.

Improve The Hamper Program
Every year the demand for our Christmas hampers increase and we want to ensure all students have access to this program. Your UMSU will adopt a new sponsorship strategy to include the wider business community to be involved with this campaign. We will also implement an improved sign-up, sponsorship, and volunteer forum to ensure the community is aware and involved with this great cause!

Mbuli Matshe | Vice-President Finance & Operations

Rebrand and Refresh the UMSU Businesses
Being an international student that has stayed in residence, I know that students deserve a place to feel comfortable on campus. Degrees and The HUB have been that place for many students, and as your VP Finance and Operations, I want to refresh these spaces by renovating the interior and revamping systems by initiating a more computerized autonomous system which will make ordering food more efficient.

Implement and Expand Participatory Budgeting
Students want a say in how their money is spent. Want Proof? Check out the hundreds of submissions you sent our way this fall as to how UMSU should spend $20,000 of our budget in our inaugural participatory budgeting initiative. Your UMSU will complete the implementation of this year’s Participatory Budgeting project, and raise the amount available to you to $50,000, which includes a $20,000 participatory budget for Bannatyne Students.

Budget Funding to Bannatyne and Inner City Support Programming System
We’ve heard from our members at Bannatyne that they would like more than a few weeks of wraps and pasta from UMSU executives. This year, we are promising to step up our game. We will implement a support program budget for our Bannatyne and Inner City campus students, where we will provide resources, programming and even more food. All UMSU members deserve the same level of support and we will make this a reality.

Advocate For Better International Healthcare Policy For The Upcoming Year
After the provincial government removed international healthcare at the end of the last academic year, the University stepped in to subsidise the cost for the first year of transition. However, international students are facing the highest fee increase with the cap set at 5% + C.P.I (last year C.P.I was 1.6%) Students are facing a higher tuition increase and Your UMSU will lobby the university to extend the subsidy period for another year to help international students during consecutive max tuition increases.

Advocate For A More Affordable UMSU Healthcare Plan
This is our final year in our current extended healthcare contract, and many students have enjoyed the access to affordable secondary healthcare the program provides; But that doesn’t mean we can’t do better. I will lead negotiations with current healthcare provider and initiate an RFP to determine the best value contract. I realise that being a student is strainias and i want to make the student experience more affordable.

Establish A Multi-Faith Space On Campus
It is evident that at this university, year after year, diversity in the demographic continues to grow. In a multicultural community it is important to have spaces and places where everyone feels safe and supported. For many students, that means we need a place where students can practice their faith on campus. That is why i will work in tandem with the university to bring a multi-faith space to campus. So no matter where you’re from or how your practice your faith, you feel like you have a home at this University.

Jelynn Dela Cruz | Vice-President Student Life

Mental Health Peer Support Call For Action
Your UMSU believes that the safety of students is a priority and on campus they deserve to feel secure in the face of crisis. This is why we will implement a call to action for our student faculty leaders to receive peer support training and encourage their student bodies to participate in Mental Health First Aid sessions facilitated by UMSU’s partnership with the Mood Disorders Association of Manitoba, available during campaign weeks. Your UMSU will also provide spaces and resources for these classes all year round, to ensure that our campus can become even more conscious in terms of support, crisis and response.

Student Group Spotlight Program & Year-End Recognition Gala
Student leaders put in endless hours into initiatives that benefit the social, academic and cultural well-being of our campus. Your UMSU wants to acknowledge their hard work by utilizing ACE meetings to vote on student group projects that deserve recognition. These groups will receive a prize, a feature on UMSU social media and a month-long slide on the University Centre televisions to help promote their accomplishments. We also want to give back to members of faculty student associations by hosting a Year-End gala to honour the amazing work that they do for our campus, and provide awards to acknowledge their work.

Improved Accessibility To Student Counselling
Your UMSU understands that when you are waiting to receive mental health counsel, the last thing that you need are long wait times. This is why we will work with the Student Counselling Centre to decrease wait time so that students can receive the help that they deserve when they need it most and to advocate for a more efficient, effective and personalized system for students.

International Student Canadian Tours During Reading Week
With family so far away, it is often tough to travel back home when you are only given a few days during breaks in the school year. This is why Your UMSU wants to improve the international student experience by partnering with travel companies to create an affordable option for you to not only explore Canada and to see what our beautiful country has to offer, but to strengthen the bonds within our intentional community more than ever.

Enhanced Peer Tutoring
Your UMSU understands that keeping up with never-ending course material can be overwhelming. This is why we will enhance your peer tutoring experience so that you don’t have to bear the burden alone. Your UMSU plans to do this by implementing Nimbus campaign days to educate students on what the service offers, through hosting pop-up speed-tutoring events as well as incentives such as contests for free sessions and a peer tutor spotlight. We will also vet and employ more students by making applications more streamlined and accessible for all.

Expanded Indigenous Awareness Campaign
Being an educational institution on Treaty 1 territory, Your UMSU believes that reconciliation and the proper informative campaigns for indigenous issues should be a priority. Working in collaboration with indigenous student groups,student leaders and the Indigenous Centre, we would provide more supports and resources to grow the month-long awareness campaign. Your UMSU wants to strengthen the impact made by devoting donations, promotional work and outreach to these important initiatives across your campus
